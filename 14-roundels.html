Roundels

Roundels are an important component in our visual identity. Use them to present key messages about our products and services and to lead a person’s eye around our content towards a call-to-action.

Anatomy of a roundel

We’ve derived the proportions of our roundels from our logo, where the sunshine circles grow by 75% each time. Roundels must be designed using this circular grid to maintain consistency between them.

The roundel grid

Roundel content examples

Roundels present important messages about our products and services. Keep roundel content succinct and highlight important content in bold, large type.

Roundel base colours

Roundels are ‘sunshine elements’ and we use them to connect important information to our brand. We use our Sunshine colour for the background of standard roundels. The content they contain is Midnight. Use Ketchup text to present price/offer related content.

Sky panels

To create sufficient colour contrast use only Sunshine and Midnight roundels.

Roundel text colours

In Sunshine roundels, use Ketchup to present price and offer information. For example “Free” or “£4.”

Accessible roundels

Our roundels have been extensively tested to ensure that they present sufficient contrast between background and text colours to help people who have visual disabilities. Only these colour combinations pass WCAG AAA.

Responsive roundels

Roundels use the Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG) format and may be scaled to any size with no loss of fidelity. Use the HTML picture element to serve the appropriate roundel for the target screen size.

Inaccessible combinations

These combinations of background and content colours are not accessible at any size and must not be used.
